Detectable Skin Conditions

Our AI can detect these 7 skin conditions

Actinic Keratosis

A rough, scaly patch caused by years of sun exposure. Early treatment is important.

Moderate Risk
Basal Cell Carcinoma

A type of skin cancer that begins in basal cells. Requires immediate medical attention.

High Risk
Benign Keratosis

A non-cancerous skin growth that appears waxy. Usually harmless but monitor for changes.

Low Risk
Dermatofibroma

A common benign fibrous nodule found on skin. Usually harmless and requires no treatment.

Low Risk
Melanoma

The most serious type of skin cancer. Immediate medical attention is required!

Very High Risk
Melanocytic Nevi

Common moles that are usually harmless. Monitor for changes in size or color.

Low Risk
Vascular Lesions

Abnormalities of blood vessels in the skin. Consult a dermatologist for treatment options.

Moderate Risk

Skin Care Tips

Follow these tips for healthy skin

Sun Protection

Always wear SPF 30+ sunscreen when going outdoors. Reapply every 2 hours for maximum protection.

Stay Hydrated

Drink at least 8 glasses of water daily. Hydration keeps skin healthy and glowing.

Regular Check-ups

Examine your skin monthly for new or changing moles. Visit a dermatologist annually for skin check.

Healthy Diet

Eat foods rich in antioxidants like fruits and vegetables. Avoid processed foods that can cause inflammation.

Get Enough Sleep

Sleep 7-8 hours daily for skin repair. Your skin regenerates while you sleep.

Avoid Smoking

Smoking accelerates skin aging and increases risk of skin cancer. Quit for healthier skin.
